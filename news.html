SURREALITY 2.0: Mixed Reality (MR) Ã— AI Art Exhibition Open Call

SURREALITY 2.0 is an upcoming Mixed Reality (MR) and AI art exhibition to be presented at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou), HKUST(GZ), and The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) in May 2026.

The exhibition series SURREALITY explores how AI and MR can reshape artistic creation and experience at an architectural and spatial scale, offering a glimpse into a future where the virtual and physical worlds seamlessly coexist. Rather than appearing as floating digital overlays, artworks in SURREALITY are anchored to physical space, maintaining spatial continuity across indoor and outdoor environments, and enabling visitors to explore a coherent MR landscape through head-mounted displays, smartphones, or tablets.


About SURREALITY 1.0

SURREALITY 2.0 builds on SURREALITY 1.0, which was presented at HKUST(GZ) from June to August 2025, featuring 31 artworks by 50 international artists, including practitioners recognized with major international awards such as the Emmy and Walkley Awards. SURREALITY 1.0 was invited as part of the 2025 Croisements Festival and received support from the Consulate General of France in Guangzhou.


Who We Invite

SURREALITY 2.0 invites artists and teams worldwide working with AI, MR, and related technologies to take part in the exhibition.


Eligible Works

SURREALITY 2.0 primarily welcome MR artworks that integrate digital content into a physical environment. Virtual Reality (VR) artworks experienced entirely within a virtual environment via a headset are also accepted.

Artworks are expected to:

  • incorporate AI into the creation process, and
  • demonstrate critical, innovative, or research-based engagement with AI

For MR artworks, artists are not required to implement on-site spatial localization or tracking systems. The exhibition team will handle MR content positioning and deployment. Artists are expected to provide digital assets for integration.


Participation & Support

Participation in SURREALITY 2.0 offers artists:

  • opportunity to develop new artwork or present existing artwork as part of a kilometer-square-scale MR exhibition
  • curatorial and technical support for the integration of artworks into the broader MR exhibition framework
  • no submission or exhibition fee
  • high visibility as a flagship project of HKUSTâ€™s 35th Anniversary celebrations, engaging audiences from the wider public, as well as artistic, academic, industry, and government communities
  • opportunities for selected artists to attend the exhibition opening and related academic events, with travel and accommodation expenses covered, subject to final arrangements

Submission Process

The open call follows a two-round submission process.

Round 1 centers on artwork information and representative media materials for curatorial review. No technical materials are required at this stage.

Shortlisted artworks will be invited to Round 2, where additional technical materials will be requested to support exhibition preparation and technical deployment.

All submissions should be made via the submission form . Please refer to the form for detailed submission requirements.


Important Dates

  • Submission Deadline (Round 1): 14 February 2026, 23:59 (UTC+8, Beijing Time)
  • Shortlist Notification: 24 February 2026
  • Submission Deadline (Round 2): 6 March 2026, 23:59 (UTC+8, Beijing Time)
  • Exhibition: May 2026

Review & Selection

Artworks will be reviewed and selected by a curatorial committee of international experts, to be announced at a later stage. Artworks will be evaluated based on artistic vision, conceptual approach, and overall feasibility within the exhibition context.


Organizers

  • Organizing Units: Center for Metaverse and Computational Creativity (MC2) & Thrust of Computational Media and Arts (CMA), The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou)
  • Supporting Units: Division of Arts and Machine Creativity (AMC) & Division of Emerging Interdisciplinary Areas (EMIA), The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology

Contact

If you have any questions or would like further information, please feel free to contact us at mc2@hkust-gz.edu.cn.
